Shout-out to Whaledrop

Dec 24th, 2009 | Comments

Just like to give a shout-out to a former project of mine that's available for public demo: whaledrop.com. Whaledrop is a hosted Digital Asset Management system for individuals and small businesses, and it's pretty dang cool. Check it out!

EDIT: The team at Whaledrop has opened up the Whaledrop Asset Courier for public beta. You can use the Whaledrop Asset Courier to send very large files and private information securely by email. You can also track the status of your email delivery on the Whaledrop web page. I use it, you should too :-).

60cycleCMS 2.5.0 released!

Dec 2nd, 2009 | Comments

At long last I've found some time to work on 60cycleCMS, and version 2.5.0 is the result. A major change in 2.5.0 is the addition of a new visitor comment system. I've integrated RECAPTCHA and HTML Purifier to thwart spammers and XSS, respectively. I've also allowed for email notifications of new comments. Of course, the comment system is both optional and configurable, meaning you can enable or disable it and its features at will.

As always, you can see the latest version in action on this very website. I've also notified the admins at opensourceCMS, and hopefully a demo of the new version will be available soon.

Download the new version from the project page or SourceForge.

Dropbox referral link

Nov 26th, 2009 | Comments

Dropbox has been around for a while, but I hadn't really been interested in it until my fiancee's laptop started making strange noises. As a result she's using my desktop to do some of her work, as well as her (possibly dying) laptop. Not to mention she's constantly emailing herself files to access them on school computers. So, a backup and sync solution seems to be in order.

Having set Dropbox up for both of us, I'm really impressed so far, especially since it works seamlessly with Linux, my platform of choice. Ars Technica has a good review of the service, and many others have jotted down their thoughts, so I'm not going to go any further than saying I like it.

If you're interested in using Dropbox, use this referral link: https://www.dropbox.com/referrals/NTMyMDkwNDc5, and we'll both get an extra 250 MB of storage!
